My Design Journey

  • 2018, going forward

    2018 is going to be the year of business and product design for me. I am working with a UC Berkeley startup, Clymb Health on the main product while finding a product market fit. This enables me to get hands on experience working on a product suite targeting multiple customers, catering to their unique needs. The challenging aspect of this project is driving a user behavior which is very tough to drive - making people enter their symptom information.

  • 2017, a year of design revelations

    In 2017 , I understood what empathy for people means and how to capture that through interviews and translate into designs . I also understood the use of channels in design and how information through different channels can be used to target different needs of users. Everything needn't be on the interface ! Also realized that the best interface is no interface. Since, ideally a system should be designed in such a way that you dont need interfaces to solve your needs and achieve your goals ! I got introduced to Clymb Health and started doing mini class projects to familiarize myself about the pains and sufferings of people who have Lyme disease.

  • 2016, Hackathons and Qualitative Exploration

    I won 3 hackathons this year. I wanted to try out newly learned design skills at the UC Berkeley School of Information and hackathons seemed to be the best way. Didn't know that the ISchool education would prove so useful that would lead me winning competitions - fresh into the country. A course on qualitative research methods in Information Systems allowed me to really understand how to design better products by interacting with people and learning about their life and the core values that drive human behavior.

  • 2015, Design Awareness and Accessibility

    This year was all about self awareness with respect to design. Having worked on all aspects of a UI, I was thinking on a broad level - a design perspective and started thinking about the people who use my products not just the stakeholders invovled in the project. Elements was my key project this year, a link to which you will find in the industry projects section. It was my first product management experience working on a garage project which ultimately received investing from key stakeholders at Microsoft.
    I also got introduced to empathy for users while working on the only E-learning tool at Microsoft, which wasn't accessible to more than 1000 specially abled people. I took up this project to understand some issues that people using this tool were facing and rolled out a version of the E-tool with the latest industy accessibility standards.

  • 2014, Design Code Develop

    Rampant Learning, Sleepless Nights are some of the phrases I will use to describe this year. I had more projects than I could handle, working on the Front-end development, from design to backend, from requirement gathering to making presentations for stakeholders and from resource allocation to attending conferences. I learnt a lot through this process, being just promoted, I understood taking more responsibilities and being accountable for them.

  • 2013, Ooh ! Design seems cool !

    I figured out how to make mobile apps during my undergraduate education and having a little art background since my childhood, got me interested in the way apps performed along with the colors, the buttons, the text etc. I joined Microsoft where I got to work on a Windows App that was used by the test vertical at Microsoft It to track project health. This was when I understood about designing a product for different types of users with different needs and it all seemed really cool !
